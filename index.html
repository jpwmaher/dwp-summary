02

The ask

We were asked by The Department for Work and Pensions to look into ways of helping their health assessors to get access to the right supporting evidence, at the right time.

Currently their difficulties in getting that information results in:

  • Anguish and frustration for claimants, eroding the trust relationship
  • More face-to-face assessments
  • Longer processing of claims
  • A high number of overtuned decisions at appeal - often with the presentation of evidence cited as a factor in the appeal decision
03

The goal

"To provide DWP's appointed assessors with access to trusted medical information"



We broke this goal down into three questions:

A. What is technically possible?
B. What information is useful?
C. What is legal?
04

The approach

Firstly we defined the users involved in current service:

Citizen
DWP healthcare professionals
GPs
Other Health Care Professionals

We spoke to citizens who have had health related benefit experience (ESA & PiP) to understand their willingness to give their consent to share their medical records with DWP.

We spoke to Health Care Professionals (focusing more on mental health) to understand their roles and involvement with submitting additional information and evidence on behalf of the citizen to support a claim.

We also spoke to DWP health benefit processing staff to understand what is useful and trusted information when making a decision.
