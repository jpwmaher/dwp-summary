C. What is legal?

We have explored the legal landscape and engaged with DWP lawyers around current powers that exist to them.

We have had confirmed that DWP does not have legal powers to get information about a claimant without consent from them and in some cases GPâ€™s.

These breakdown to the following:

  • Is consent needed from a claimant to get information from a health system in a real time application - No. The action of them making application is enough
  • Is consent needed from a GP to get information from a health system on the claimants behalf in a real time application? - Yes
  • Is consent needed from a claimant to get information from a health system on their behalf after an application has been submitted - Yes
  • Is consent needed from a HP to get information from a health system on the claimants behalf after an application has been submitted - Yes

Consent from a GP is not needed if a data sharing agreement is in place with the appropriate governance bodies. This will vary from system to system, these bodies are listed in the data catalogue.
