A. What is techincally possible?

It must be noted that none of the technical possiblities can be achieved without first forming a partnership between DWP and NHS X, NHS England and NHS Digital. It’s important that all departments agree to help users get the support they need from DWP in a consent driven service.


DWP asked us to understand what are technically possible ways DWP could access health data currently and in the near future. We have used openly available test environments to create API’s for systems (where possible).

These are the systems and next steps DWP would need to take to enable access:

  • Within 1 year
  • Within 2 years
  • Within 5 years

SCRa is expanding its data and will at some point include GP Connect and NRL retrieval. They are currently seeking input from current and future users of the system to inform it’s roadmap. This could prove to be a good opportunity for DWP to influence NHS strategy.

To access this system DWP would need to engage with SCRa team. This service operates on a user consent based model for access to the SCR.

DWP would require to also onboard to NHS identity or look at reuse of the DS1500 technology built in DD/MM/YYYY.

GP Connect


Data currently held within GP Connect:

Data item Explanation
Care Plan A healthcare plan for an individual
Condition Detail of a patient's condition, problems or diagnosis
Dosage Medication dosage
Medication Purpose of prescribed medication

To access this system DWP would need to engage NHS England & NHS X to agree a data sharing agreement, carry out a data protection impact assessment and appoint a clinical safety officer who can assure the system is safe to use.

DWP would also require to onboard to Spine Mini Service Provider (SMSP) to enable access to GP Connect.

Within 5 years

National Record Locator (NRL)


Data currently held within GP Connect:

Data item Explanation
Care Plan An individual's mental healthcare only

The NRL can help get access to mental healthcare plans that exist outside of primary care (GP’s and Hospitals) that may not have been shared.

To access this system DWP would need to complete an assessment. The assessment will look at a range of criteria including technical conformance, data protection, security, clinical safety, benefits measurement, business process review and updates and service operations.
